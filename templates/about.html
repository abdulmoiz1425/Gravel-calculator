Our Approach

How We Handle Estimates

Gravel weight and coverage can change based on material size, moisture, compaction, ground conditions, and installation method. Because of these factors, no online calculator can guarantee the exact amount required for every project.

We recommend adding a small extra amount for compaction, settling, uneven ground, and waste. Users should also confirm the final order quantity with a local gravel supplier, contractor, or project professional.

Factors That Affect Estimates
Material Size

Larger stone sizes leave more air gaps, affecting actual coverage per yard.

Moisture Content

Wet gravel is heavier than dry gravel, which affects ton estimates from suppliers.

Compaction

Compacted gravel layers take more material than loosely spread stone at the same depth.

Ground Conditions

Slopes, low spots, and uneven surfaces require more material than flat, level areas.

Installation Method

Hand-spreading, machine spreading, and grading all affect how much material is used.

Our results are planning estimates. Always confirm final quantities with a local supplier or contractor.
