What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used by website owners to make websites work, or to work more efficiently, as well as to provide reporting information.

Cookies set by the website owner (in this case, Gravel Calculator Pro) are called "first-party cookies." Cookies set by parties other than the website owner are called "third-party cookies." Third-party cookies enable third-party features or functionality to be provided on or through the website (such as advertising, interactive content, and analytics).

Cookies can remain on your computer or mobile device for different periods of time. Some cookies are "session cookies," meaning they exist only while your browser is open and are deleted automatically once you close your browser. Other cookies are "persistent cookies," meaning they survive after your browser is closed and can be used by websites to recognize your computer when you open your browser and browse the internet again.

How We Use Cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies for the following purposes:

  • Website Functionality: To enable core website features and ensure the website works properly across all devices and browsers.
  • Analytics and Performance: To understand how visitors interact with our website, including which pages are visited most often, how long visitors stay, and where they come from.
  • User Preferences: To remember your preferences and settings (such as calculator units or previously entered values) so you don't have to re-enter them each visit.
  • Security: To protect our website and users from fraudulent activity, spam, and abuse.
  • Advertising: To serve relevant advertisements and to measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns.
  • Affiliate Tracking: To track referrals from affiliate partners and ensure commissions are properly attributed.
  • Social Media Integration: To enable social sharing functionality and embed social media content.
  • Content Delivery: To deliver content efficiently through content delivery networks (CDNs).
  • Error Monitoring: To detect and diagnose technical problems and errors on our website.

Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies

Required for the website to function correctly. These cannot be disabled as the website would not work without them. They include session management and security cookies.

Analytics Cookies

Help us understand how visitors use our site. All data is anonymized. Provided by Google Analytics 4 to track page views, sessions, and user behavior.

Functionality Cookies

Enable enhanced functionality and personalization, such as remembering your preferred units (feet vs. meters) or previously used calculator settings.

Advertising Cookies

Used by Google AdSense and other ad networks to serve relevant advertisements based on your interests and browsing history across the web.

Affiliate Cookies

Track referrals to affiliate partner websites (such as gravel suppliers or Amazon) to attribute purchases and ensure proper commission payments.

Third-Party Cookies

In addition to our own cookies, we allow the following third-party services to set cookies on your device when you visit our website:

  • Google Analytics 4: Website analytics and traffic measurement. Cookies include _ga, _ga_*, and _gid.
  • Google AdSense / Google Ad Manager: Display advertising and ad targeting. Cookies include IDE, DSID, and various Google ad cookies.
  • Google Tag Manager: Tag management system used to deploy analytics and advertising tags. Sets its own cookies for tag management purposes.
  • Cloudflare: CDN, performance, and security services. May set cookies for security verification and bot protection (e.g., __cf_bm, __cfduid).
  • Amazon Associates: Affiliate tracking for Amazon product links. Sets cookies to track referrals and attribute purchases.
  • DoubleClick (Google): Interest-based advertising and remarketing. Sets cookies to track users across websites for ad targeting.
  • YouTube: If our site embeds any YouTube videos, YouTube may set cookies related to video playback and user preferences.

These third-party services have their own privacy and cookie policies, which we encourage you to review. We do not control these third-party cookies.

Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to analyze the use of our website. Google Analytics uses cookies to collect information about website usage, such as:

  • The number of users visiting the website
  • Which pages are most frequently visited
  • How long visitors spend on each page
  • Which websites referred visitors to our site
  • The geographic location of visitors (country/region level)
  • Device and browser type
Google Analytics data is anonymized. Individual users cannot be identified. We have enabled IP anonymization to further protect your privacy.

You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking by installing the free Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on. This add-on prevents the Google Analytics JavaScript (ga.js, analytics.js, and dc.js) from sharing information with Google Analytics about visit activity.

Google Advertising Services

We participate in Google's advertising programs, including Google AdSense and Google Ad Manager. These services allow Google to display advertisements on our website that may be targeted to you based on your browsing history and interests.

Google uses cookies such as the NID cookie to deliver these ads, personalize their content, and measure their performance. Google may also use these cookies for remarketing, showing you ads on other websites after you have visited ours.

You have several options to control Google advertising cookies:

  • Visit Google Ads Settings to manage personalized ad preferences
  • Install the Google Ads Opt-out Extension for your browser
  • Visit aboutads.info to opt out of interest-based advertising from multiple companies

Your Cookie Choices

You can control and manage cookies in several ways. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website:

Google Chrome Settings → Privacy → Cookies
Mozilla Firefox Options → Privacy & Security
Apple Safari Preferences → Privacy
Microsoft Edge Settings → Cookies & Site Permissions
Opera Browser Settings → Advanced → Privacy
Brave Browser Settings → Shields → Cookies

In addition to browser controls, you can opt out of specific advertising and analytics cookies using the opt-out tools provided in the sections above. Please note that opting out of advertising cookies does not mean you will no longer see ads. It means the ads you see will be less relevant to your interests.

Cookie Consent Settings

When you first visit our website, you may be presented with a cookie consent notice that allows you to accept or decline non-essential cookies. You can change your cookie preferences at any time by:

  • Using your browser's cookie management settings to delete or block specific cookies
  • Clearing all cookies from your browser and revisiting our site to make a new selection
  • Using the third-party opt-out tools described in the sections above for specific services

Please note that essential cookies cannot be disabled through our cookie consent tool as they are required for the website to function properly.

Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites that you do not want to have your online activities tracked. At this time, our website does not currently respond to DNT browser signals, as there is no consistent industry standard for how to interpret and respond to such signals.

However, you can use the opt-out tools described in this Cookie Policy to limit tracking by specific third-party services. We continue to monitor developments in DNT standards and may update our practices accordingly.
